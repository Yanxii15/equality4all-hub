Argument reference · Palestine

Palestine, occupation, and consistent standards.

A clean argument map: civilian life matters, occupation has legal consequences, and self-determination is a right.

The basic position: supporting Palestine means supporting Palestinian self-determination, opposing occupation and settlement expansion, and refusing to treat Palestinian civilians as disposable background damage.

It does not require hating Jews. It does not require defending the killing of Israeli civilians. And it does not require pretending every Palestinian faction is morally pure. The point is simpler: rights do not vanish because the politics are messy.

Precision

Criticise states, armies, policies, and political movements. Do not turn that into hatred of Jews as a people.

Humanity

Every death matters. Palestinians and Israelis are not debate props.

Consistency

If occupation, collective punishment, or targeting civilians is wrong elsewhere, it is wrong here too.
